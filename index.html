Demonstrating MOSART: Opening
Articulated Objects in the Real World

This paper presents the design and evaluation of MOSART, a system for opening novel articulated objects in novel environments. MOSART adopts a modular approach. It employs state-of-the-art modules for perception (a MaskRCNN that predicts articulation parameters) and planning (SeqIK, a trajectory optimization framework to generate whole body motion plans) and uses proprioceptive feedback for precise execution. MOSART enables a commodity mobile manipulator (Stretch RE2) to open novel prismatic-jointed drawers and vertical-hinged cabinets in novel environments. In extensive real world testing on 31 different drawers and cabinets across 13 different real world environments, MOSART achieves a 61% success rate while convincingly outperforming imitation learning approaches trained on a similar amount of data. Furthermore, the modular design enables quick extension of MOSART to other articulation types (horizontal-hinged ovens). Detailed analysis sheds light on what makes this problem hard and the major bottlenecks of the current system. Please refer to the supplementary material for videos and code.
